Detailed Description

Allows Jmax (at 25 degrees C) to vary based on development index.

Here we wish to specify separate values of \( J_{max} \) (at 25 degrees C) for mature and emerging plants, where the value for mature plants is \( J_{max}^{mature} \) and the value for emerging plants is \( J_{max}^{emerging} = f_{emerging} \cdot J_{max}^{mature} \). Here \( f_{emerging} \) is a scaling factor that is typically less than 1; in other words, \( J_{max}^{mature} \) is typically larger than \( J_{max}^{emerging} \).

We would like to specify a value of \( DVI \) where this transition occurs ( \( DVI_0 \)), and we wish the transition to be sudden but continuous. One way to accomplish this is to use a logistic function with a specified transition width \( \Delta_{DVI} \):

\[ J_{max}(DVI) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k \cdot (DVI - DVI_0) / \Delta_{DVI}}} + C \qquad \text{Equation (1)} \]

Here \( L \), \( k \), and \( C \) are constants whose values we will soon determine.

When \( DVI \) approaches \( - \infty \), \( J_{max}(DVI) \) approaches \( C \). This corresponds to the limit of small \( DVI \), where the plant should be in the emerging stage. From this, we can see that \( C = J_{max}^{emerging} \).

When \( DVI \) approaches \( \infty \), \( J_{max}(DVI) \) approaches \( L + C \). This corresponds to the limit of large \( DVI \), where the plant should be in the mature stage. From this, we can see that \( L + C = J_{max}^{mature} \).

With this information, we can now update Equation (1) to

\[ J_{max}(DVI) = (1 - f_{emerging}) \cdot J_{max}^{mature} \cdot \frac{1}{1 + e^{-k \cdot (DVI - DVI_0) / \Delta_{DVI}}} + f_{emerging} \cdot J_{max}^{mature} \qquad \text{Equation (2)} \]

Using Equation (2) we can see that \( J_{max}(DVI_0) = J_{max}^{mature} \cdot (1 + f_{emerging}) \), which is the average of \( J_{max}^{mature} \) and \( J_{max}^{emerging} \). Thus, \( DVI_0 \) marks the midpoint of the transition. As \( DVI \) increases beyond \( DVI_0 \), \( J_{max}(DVI) \) gets closer and closer to the mature value. If we want to specify that the transition is 95% complete when \( DVI = DVI_0 + \Delta_{DVI} \), then we can choose \( k = - ln(1 / 0.95 - 1) \). (We omit the math here, but it is straightforward to arrive at this answer.)

Now, we can update the equation one more time:

\[ J_{max}(DVI) = (1 - f_{emerging}) \cdot J_{max}^{mature} \cdot \frac{1}{1 + e^{-ln(1 / 0.95 - 1) \cdot (DVI - DVI_0) / \Delta_{DVI}}} + f_{emerging} \cdot J_{max}^{mature} \qquad \text{Equation (3)} \]

This is the desired function that switches continuously between the two values, where the transition is centered at \( DVI_0 \) and has a width of \( \Delta_{DVI} \) (in the sense described above).

This module implements Equation (3) where the values of \( J_{max}^{mature} \), \( DVI_0 \), \( \Delta_{DVI} \) and \( f_{emerging} \) are taken from the BioCro quantities called 'jmax_mature', 'DVI0_jmax', 'Delta_DVI_jmax', and 'sf_jmax', respectively.
